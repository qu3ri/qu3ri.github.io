About Me


Hi, I am Kyri Lea, a 4th year BS/MS Computing Security student at Rochester Institute of Technology. I enjoy participating in Red vs Blue and Pentesting competitions, like ISTS and CPTC. My primary interests are Active Directory penetration testing, data privacy, and all things Linux.

In my free time, I like to go bouldering, do gymnastics, go shopping. I am the secretary for RIT's Gymnastics Club and the treasurer for the Linux Users Group (RITlug). As part of RITlug, I have given presentations on Intro to the Linux Command Line, Pluggable Authentication Modules (PAM), Pentesting Linux Systems, and Mainframes.

I have five summers of experience working in the cybersecurity field. I spent four summers at Digi-Key Electronics working with the security team on data privacy, application security, logging and monitoring systems, and identity and access management. Most recently, I interned on IBM's X-Force Red and researched security vulnerabilities associated with smart gym equipment. I am currently working for X-Force Red as a co-op penetration tester.
